Facts About Icecream

Pizza originated in Italy, with its modern form becoming popular in Naples during the 18th century. The classic pizza consists of a dough base, tomato sauce, cheese, and various toppings like pepperoni, vegetables, or seafood. Today, it's one of the most beloved foods worldwide, with variations that differ by region, such as the thin-crust pizza in New York and the deep-dish pizza in Chicago. The largest pizza ever made was over 1261 square meters, created in Rome in 2012!

Pizza's global popularity exploded in the 20th century, especially after World War II, when American soldiers stationed in Italy brought the dish back to the U.S. Over time, pizza chains like Domino's and Pizza Hut revolutionized how it was delivered, further boosting its accessibility. In addition to the classic Margherita, there are countless varieties. For example, in Japan, pizzas can be topped with unique ingredients like squid or teriyaki chicken. In Italy, there's a tradition of enjoying pizza with family or friends in a leisurely setting. The dough itself has evolved, with gluten-free and cauliflower crust options available to cater to dietary needs. Pizza also holds the title of a food with countless creative twists, from dessert pizzas topped with chocolate and fruits to vegan alternatives for the cheese.
